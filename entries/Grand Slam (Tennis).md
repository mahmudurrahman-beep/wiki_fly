## Introduction
A **Grand Slam** in tennis refers to the four major tournaments: the **Australian Open**, **French Open**, **Wimbledon**, and the **US Open**. These are the most prestigious events in the sport, played annually on different surfaces.

## History / Origins
- The term originated in **1933**, when journalist John Kieran compared winning all four majors to scoring a "grand slam" in bridge.  
- The first player to achieve a **Calendar Grand Slam** was **Don Budge** in 1938.  
- Rod Laver remains the only player to win it twice (1962 and 1969).
