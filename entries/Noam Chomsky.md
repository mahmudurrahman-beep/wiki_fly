## Early Life
- Born: **December 7, 1928**, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA.  
- Education: University of Pennsylvania (BA, MA, PhD).  
- Doctoral thesis: *Transformational Analysis* (1955).  
- Influenced by linguist **Zellig Harris**.  

## Academic Contributions
- **Linguistics**: Revolutionized the field with *Syntactic Structures* (1957).  
- Developed the theory of **generative grammar**, showing how humans produce language from innate structures.  
- Proposed the concept of a **universal grammar** — the idea that all human languages share a common underlying structure.  

## Political Activism
- Vocal critic of U.S. foreign policy, corporate power, and media manipulation.  
- Co‑authored *Manufacturing Consent* (1988) with Edward S. Herman, analyzing media bias.  
- Advocates for **anarcho‑syndicalism** and **libertarian socialism**.  

## Major Works
- *Syntactic Structures* (1957)  
- *The Responsibility of Intellectuals* (1967)  
- *Manufacturing Consent* (1988)  
- *How the World Works* (2011)  
- *Requiem for the American Dream* (2017)  

## Legacy
- Known as the **most cited living author** in the humanities.  
- His linguistic theories reshaped psychology, philosophy, and computer science.  
- His political writings continue to inspire debates on democracy, media, and global justice.  

---

*Noam Chomsky remains a towering figure in both linguistics and political thought, bridging science and activism in ways few intellectuals have achieved.*
