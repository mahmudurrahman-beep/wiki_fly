## Introduction
**Leninism** is a political ideology developed by **Vladimir Lenin**, building upon Marxist theory. It emphasizes the role of a **vanguard party** to lead the working class in overthrowing capitalism and establishing a dictatorship of the proletariat as a step toward communism.

## History / Origins
- Emerged during the early 20th century in the context of the Russian Empire.  
- Lenin expanded Marxist ideas to address conditions in Russia, focusing on imperialism, revolution, and party organization.  
- Played a central role in the **Russian Revolution of 1917**, leading to the establishment of the Soviet Union.  

## Key Features
- **Vanguard Party**: A disciplined, centralized party to provide leadership and revolutionary consciousness to the working class.  
- **Democratic Centralism**: Internal party democracy combined with strict unity in action.  
- **Imperialism Theory**: Lenin argued imperialism was the highest stage of capitalism, leading to global conflict.  
- **Dictatorship of the Proletariat**: Transitional state power led by workers to dismantle capitalist structures.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Leninism became the foundation of **Marxism-Leninism**, the official ideology of the Soviet Union.  
- Influenced revolutionary movements worldwide, including China, Cuba, and Vietnam.  
- Continues to shape leftist political thought and debates on socialism and communism.
