## Origins
- Founded: **April 5, 1993** in Sunnyvale, California.  
- Founders: **Jensen Huang**, **Chris Malachowsky**, **Curtis Priem**.  
- Headquarters: **Santa Clara, California, USA**.  
- Publicly traded: **NASDAQ: NVDA**.  

## Key Products & Technologies
- **GeForce GPUs** – dominant in gaming and consumer graphics.  
- **NVIDIA RTX Series** – real-time ray tracing and AI-powered graphics (DLSS).  
- **Data Center GPUs** – powering AI, machine learning, and cloud computing.  
- **CUDA Platform** – parallel computing architecture widely used in AI research.  
- **NVIDIA Omniverse** – 3D simulation and collaboration platform.  
- **NVIDIA DRIVE** – autonomous vehicle computing systems.  

## Financials (FY2025)
- Revenue: **US$130.5 billion**.  
- Operating income: **US$81.5 billion**.  
- Net income: **US$72.9 billion**.  
- Employees: ~**36,000** worldwide.  

## Global Expansion
- Major R&D centers in the U.S., Europe, and Asia.  
- In **2025**, Nvidia announced a **160,000 m² R&D campus in Kiryat Tivon, Israel**, expected to create **10,000 jobs** by 2031.  

## Market Role
- Nvidia dominates the **AI computing market**, especially since the AI boom began in 2023.  
- Competes with AMD, Intel, and emerging custom chipmakers.  
- GPUs are critical for **AI training, gaming, and scientific research**.  

## Legacy & Importance
- Transformed from a gaming GPU company into a **global AI powerhouse**.  
- Its chips power everything from **ChatGPT-like AI models** to **self-driving cars**.  
- CEO **Jensen Huang** is widely recognized as a visionary in AI and computing.
