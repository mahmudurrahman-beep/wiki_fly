## Definition
- A pyramid is a structure with a polygonal base (often square) and triangular sides meeting at an apex.  
- The design distributes weight evenly, allowing ancient civilizations to build massive monuments.  

## Egyptian Pyramids
- Built during Egypt’s Old and Middle Kingdoms (c. 2700–1700 BCE).  
- Served as tombs for pharaohs and elites.  
- The **Pyramids of Giza** (Khufu, Khafre, Menkaure) are the most famous.  
- The Great Pyramid of Khufu is the **oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World** and the only one still standing.  

## Other Civilizations
- **Mesopotamia:** Ziggurats (stepped pyramids used as temples).  
- **Mesoamerica:** Mayan and Aztec pyramids (e.g., Chichen Itza, Teotihuacan) used for rituals and sacrifices.  
- **Cambodia:** Koh Ker pyramid temple.  

## Cultural Significance
- Symbolized religious beliefs, power, and connection to the divine.  
- In Egypt, pyramids reflected the pharaoh’s role as mediator between gods and people.  
- In Mesoamerica, pyramids were central to ceremonial life.  

## Modern Importance
- Pyramids remain iconic symbols of ancient engineering.  
- Attract millions of tourists annually.  
- Continue to inspire architectural and cultural imagination.
