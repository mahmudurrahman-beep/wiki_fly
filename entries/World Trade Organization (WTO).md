## Definition
The **World Trade Organization (WTO)** is the only global international organization dealing with the rules of trade between nations. Its goal is to ensure that trade flows smoothly, predictably, and freely.

## Origins
- Founded: **1 January 1995**, replacing the **General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT)**.  
- Headquarters: **Centre William Rappard, Geneva, Switzerland**.  
- Membership: **166 members** (including the European Union, Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan).  

## Functions
- **Trade Negotiations** – Provides a forum for negotiating trade agreements.  
- **Dispute Settlement** – Resolves trade disputes between member countries.  
- **Monitoring** – Reviews national trade policies to ensure compliance.  
- **Capacity Building** – Assists developing countries in trade policy and participation.  

## Principles
- **Non‑discrimination** – Most‑favored‑nation (MFN) treatment ensures equal trade opportunities.  
- **Reciprocity** – Members exchange concessions fairly.  
- **Transparency** – Trade policies must be clear and published.  
- **Safety Valves** – Allows exceptions for environmental protection, national security, or emergencies.  

## Leadership
- Current Director‑General: **Ngozi Okonjo‑Iweala** (since 2021).  
- Official languages: **English, French, Spanish**.  
- Budget (2024): **CHF 204.9 million**.  

## Importance
- Facilitates global trade by reducing tariffs and barriers.  
- Provides stability and predictability in international commerce.  
- Helps integrate developing countries into the global economy.  
- Plays a role in addressing modern challenges like digital trade and sustainability.  

---

*The WTO is central to global trade governance, balancing free trade with fairness, and remains a key institution in shaping economic globalization.*
