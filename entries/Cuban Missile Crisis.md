## Causes
- After the **Bay of Pigs invasion (1961)**, Cuba sought protection from the USSR.  
- The Soviet Union, led by **Nikita Khrushchev**, secretly deployed nuclear missiles in Cuba.  
- U.S. reconnaissance flights discovered missile sites in October 1962.  

## Key Events
- **October 16, 1962:** President **John F. Kennedy** informed of missiles in Cuba.  
- **October 22:** Kennedy announced a **naval blockade (“quarantine”)** of Cuba.  
- **October 24–25:** Soviet ships approached but did not break the blockade.  
- **October 27:** A U.S. U-2 spy plane was shot down over Cuba, heightening tensions.  
- **October 28:** Khrushchev agreed to remove missiles in exchange for U.S. pledges not to invade Cuba and secretly removing U.S. missiles from Turkey.  

## Resolution
- Public outcome: Soviet missiles withdrawn from Cuba.  
- Secret deal: U.S. removed **Jupiter missiles from Turkey and Italy**.  
- A **direct hotline** between Washington and Moscow was established to prevent future crises.  

## Significance
- Marked the **peak of Cold War tensions**.  
- Demonstrated the dangers of nuclear brinkmanship.  
- Strengthened Kennedy’s leadership image.  
- Led to future arms control agreements, including the **Nuclear Test Ban Treaty (1963)**.
