## Geography
- Location: Spreads across **Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Bolivia, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana**.  
- Area: **5.5 million km² (2.1 million sq mi)**.  
- Contains the **Amazon River Basin**, the largest drainage system in the world.  

## Biodiversity
- Estimated **390 billion trees** belonging to 16,000 species.  
- Home to **10% of known species on Earth**.  
- Iconic animals: jaguars, sloths, macaws, poison dart frogs, pink river dolphins.  
- Over **2.5 million insect species** identified.  

## Climate & Ecology
- Hot, humid, equatorial climate.  
- Average rainfall: **2,000–3,000 mm annually**.  
- Plays a critical role in regulating global climate and carbon storage.  

## Indigenous Peoples
- Around **400–500 indigenous groups** live in the Amazon.  
- Many communities rely on traditional knowledge for sustainable living.  
- Some tribes remain uncontacted, preserving ancient ways of life.  

## Threats
- **Deforestation** from logging, cattle ranching, and agriculture.  
- **Mining and oil extraction** damaging ecosystems.  
- Climate change risks: parts of the forest are turning into savannah.  
- Lost forest cover threatens biodiversity and global climate stability.  

## Conservation
- Protected areas and reserves established across Amazon countries.  
- International efforts (WWF, UN-REDD, local NGOs) focus on reforestation and indigenous rights.  
- Recent initiatives aim to balance economic development with ecological preservation.
