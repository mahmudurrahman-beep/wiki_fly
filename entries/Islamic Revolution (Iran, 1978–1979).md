## Background
- Iran under **Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi** (1941–1979) was marked by modernization, Westernization, and authoritarian rule.  
- Discontent grew due to **political repression**, **economic inequality**, **corruption**, and **Western influence**.  
- Religious leaders, students, workers, and intellectuals united against the Shah.  

## Causes
- **Authoritarian rule** and suppression of opposition.  
- **Economic problems** despite oil wealth.  
- **Social injustice** and widening gap between rich and poor.  
- **Exile of Ayatollah Khomeini**, who became a symbol of resistance.  
- Anger at U.S. support for the Shah’s regime.  

## Key Events
- **January 1978**: Protests began after a government article insulted Khomeini.  
- **Late 1978**: Mass demonstrations, strikes, and civil resistance paralyzed the country.  
- **February 1, 1979**: Khomeini returned from exile in France to Tehran.  
- **February 11, 1979**: The Shah’s regime collapsed; revolutionaries declared victory.  

## Results
- End of the **Pahlavi dynasty**.  
- Establishment of the **Islamic Republic of Iran** under Ayatollah Khomeini.  
- Introduction of **Sharia‑based governance**.  
- Shift in global politics: Iran became anti‑Western and anti‑U.S. in orientation.  

## Legacy
- Inspired Islamist movements worldwide.  
- Led to decades of tension between Iran and the West.  
- Marked a turning point in Middle Eastern geopolitics.
