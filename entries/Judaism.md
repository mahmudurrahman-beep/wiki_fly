## Definition
**Judaism** is the religion, philosophy, and way of life of the Jewish people. It is one of the oldest monotheistic faiths, centered on the belief in one God and guided by the teachings of the **Torah**.

## Origins
- Emerged in the ancient Near East over **3,000 years ago**.  
- Rooted in the covenant between God and the patriarch **Abraham**.  
- Developed through the leadership of figures like **Moses**, who received the Torah at Mount Sinai.  

## Core Beliefs
- **Monotheism** – Belief in one, indivisible God.  
- **Covenant** – A sacred agreement between God and the Jewish people.  
- **Law (Halakha)** – Religious and ethical rules derived from the Torah and rabbinic tradition.  
- **Messianic Hope** – Belief in the coming of the Messiah and future redemption.  

## Sacred Texts
- **Torah** – The first five books of the Hebrew Bible.  
- **Tanakh** – The Hebrew Bible (Torah, Prophets, Writings).  
- **Talmud** – Rabbinic discussions and interpretations of Jewish law.  

## Practices
- **Prayer** – Daily prayers, often in Hebrew.  
- **Sabbath (Shabbat)** – Weekly day of rest from Friday evening to Saturday evening.  
- **Festivals** – Passover, Yom Kippur, Rosh Hashanah, Hanukkah, Sukkot, Purim.  
- **Dietary Laws** – Kosher food practices.  

## Branches
- **Orthodox Judaism** – Strict adherence to traditional law.  
- **Conservative Judaism** – Balances tradition with modernity.  
- **Reform Judaism** – Emphasizes ethical teachings and adapts practices to modern life.  

## Legacy
- Judaism has profoundly influenced Christianity and Islam.  
- Jewish culture, ethics, and scholarship continue to shape philosophy, law, and society.  
- Today, Judaism is practiced worldwide, with major communities in Israel, the United States, and Europe.  

---

*Judaism is both a religion and a cultural identity, rooted in ancient tradition yet continually evolving across history.*
