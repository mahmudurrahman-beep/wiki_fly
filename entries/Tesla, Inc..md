## Origins
- Founded: **July 1, 2003**, in San Carlos, California.  
- Founders: **Martin Eberhard** and **Marc Tarpenning**.  
- Elon Musk joined soon after, becoming the largest investor and later CEO.  
- Originally named **Tesla Motors**, renamed **Tesla, Inc.** in 2017.  

## Headquarters & Operations
- Headquarters: **Gigafactory Texas**, near Austin.  
- Global presence: 1,359 sales/service centers, 7,791 Supercharger stations worldwide.  
- Area served: North America, Europe, East Asia, Middle East, Oceania, Southeast Asia, Indian Subcontinent.  

## Products
- **Electric Vehicles**: Model S, Model 3, Model X, Model Y, Cybertruck, Semi, Roadster.  
- **Energy Solutions**: Powerwall, Megapack, Solar Roof, solar panels.  
- **Services**: Charging, insurance, maintenance.  

## Production & Financials
- Vehicles produced in 2024: **1,773,443 units**.  
- Battery energy storage output: **31.4 GWh** in 2024.  
- Revenue (2024): **US$97 billion**.  
- Tesla stock: traded on **NASDAQ: TSLA**.  

## Innovations
- Pioneered **autonomous driving technology** with Autopilot and Full Self‑Driving (FSD).  
- Expanded **Supercharger network** for fast EV charging.  
- Developing **robotaxis** and AI‑powered mobility solutions.  

## Legacy
- Tesla is accelerating the world’s transition to **sustainable energy**.  
- Considered the most influential EV company, reshaping the auto industry.  
- Continues to push boundaries in AI, robotics, and clean energy.
