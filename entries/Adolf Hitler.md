## Early Life
- Born: **20 April 1889**, Braunau am Inn, Austria-Hungary.  
- Moved to Vienna; developed nationalist and anti-Semitic views.  
- Served as a soldier in **World War I**, wounded and decorated.  

## Rise to Power
- Joined the **German Workers’ Party (DAP)** in 1919, later renamed the **Nazi Party (NSDAP)**.  
- Attempted coup: **Beer Hall Putsch (1923)**, failed, imprisoned.  
- Wrote *Mein Kampf* in prison, outlining his ideology.  
- Exploited economic crisis and resentment over the Treaty of Versailles.  
- Appointed **Chancellor of Germany in 1933**.  
- Consolidated power through the **Enabling Act**, becoming dictator.  

## Nazi Germany (Third Reich)
- Established a **totalitarian regime**.  
- Policies: militarization, suppression of opposition, propaganda, racial laws.  
- Aggressive expansion: annexed Austria (1938), invaded Poland (1939).  
- Triggered **World War II (1939–1945)**.  

## Atrocities
- **Holocaust:** Systematic genocide of Jews, Roma, disabled people, and political opponents.  
- Millions killed in concentration and extermination camps.  
- War crimes across occupied Europe.  

## Downfall
- Defeats on Eastern and Western fronts (Stalingrad, Normandy).  
- Allied forces closed in on Berlin in April 1945.  
- Hitler died by suicide on **30 April 1945** in his Berlin bunker.  
- Germany surrendered on **8 May 1945**.  

## Legacy
- Remembered as one of history’s most destructive figures.  
- His regime caused **tens of millions of deaths**.  
- Led to postwar efforts to prevent genocide and establish international human rights laws.
