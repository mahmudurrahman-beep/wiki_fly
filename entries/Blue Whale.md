# Blue Whale (*Balaenoptera musculus*)

## Definition
The **Blue Whale** is the largest animal on Earth, a marine mammal belonging to the baleen whale family. It can reach lengths of over **30 meters (98 feet)** and weigh up to **180 metric tons**.

## Physical Characteristics
- **Length:** 24–30 meters (80–98 feet).  
- **Weight:** 100–180 metric tons.  
- **Coloration:** Bluish-gray with lighter spots; underside paler.  
- **Heart size:** About the size of a small car (~180 kg).  
- **Tongue weight:** ~4 tons, capable of holding an elephant’s weight.  

## Habitat & Distribution
- Found in all major oceans except the Arctic.  
- Prefer deep ocean waters over coastal regions.  
- Migrate seasonally: feed in polar waters during summer, breed in tropical waters during winter.  

## Diet
- Feed primarily on **krill** (tiny shrimp-like crustaceans).  
- Consume up to **4 tons of krill per day** during feeding season.  
- Filter food using **baleen plates** instead of teeth.  

## Behavior
- Generally solitary or found in small groups.  
- Communicate using **low-frequency vocalizations**, among the loudest sounds made by any animal.  
- Can live up to **80–90 years**.  

## Reproduction
- Gestation: ~11 months.  
- Calves: Born ~7 meters long, weighing ~2–3 tons.  
- Calves nurse for ~6–7 months, gaining ~90 kg per day.  

## Conservation Status
- Listed as **Endangered** by the IUCN.  
- Population declined drastically due to **commercial whaling (19th–20th centuries)**.  
- Current global population: estimated **10,000–25,000 individuals**.  
- Threats: ship strikes, climate change, ocean noise pollution, and declining krill populations.  

## Legacy
The Blue Whale symbolizes the **majesty and fragility of marine life**. Despite its enormous size, it depends on tiny krill, showing the delicate balance of ocean ecosystems.
