## Introduction
Stephen Hawking (1942â€“2018) was a British theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author. He became one of the most famous scientists of his time, known for his groundbreaking work on black holes and the nature of the universe, as well as his ability to communicate complex ideas to the public.

## History / Origins
- Born on **8 January 1942** in Oxford, England.  
- Studied at **University College, Oxford** (BA) and **Trinity Hall, Cambridge** (PhD).  
- Diagnosed with **amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)** at age 21, which gradually paralyzed him.  
- Despite his condition, he continued his research and teaching at the University of Cambridge.  

## Key Contributions
- Proposed that **black holes emit radiation**, now known as **Hawking radiation**.  
- Advanced theories on cosmology, general relativity, and quantum gravity.  
- Authored the bestseller **A Brief History of Time (1988)**, which sold millions of copies worldwide.  
- Advocated for science communication, appearing in documentaries, lectures, and even pop culture (e.g., *Star Trek*, *The Simpsons*).  

## Legacy / Impact
- Regarded as one of the greatest scientific minds of the modern era.  
- Inspired millions by overcoming physical challenges while contributing to science.  
- His work continues to influence research in physics and cosmology.  
- Buried at **Westminster Abbey**, near Isaac Newton and Charles Darwin.
