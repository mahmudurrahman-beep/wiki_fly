# Friedrich Nietzsche

## Early Life
- Born: **October 15, 1844**, Röcken, Prussia.  
- Studied classical philology at the University of Leipzig.  
- Became a professor at the University of Basel at age 24.  
- Resigned due to health issues, dedicating himself to writing.  

## Key Philosophical Ideas
- **Death of God:** Traditional religion and metaphysics are no longer credible in modern society.  
- **Übermensch (Overman):** A higher type of human who creates values beyond conventional morality.  
- **Will to Power:** Fundamental drive in humans, beyond survival or reproduction.  
- **Eternal Recurrence:** The idea that life repeats infinitely, challenging individuals to affirm existence.  
- **Perspectivism:** Truth is not absolute but depends on perspective.  

## Major Works
- *The Birth of Tragedy* (1872) – on Greek tragedy and art.  
- *Thus Spoke Zarathustra* (1883–85) – introduces the Übermensch.  
- *Beyond Good and Evil* (1886) – critiques traditional morality.  
- *On the Genealogy of Morality* (1887) – explores the origins of moral values.  

## Later Life
- Suffered a mental collapse in 1889, possibly due to syphilis or other illness.  
- Spent his final years incapacitated, cared for by his mother and sister.  
- Died: **August 25, 1900**, Weimar, Germany.  

## Legacy
- Influenced existentialists like Sartre and Camus, psychoanalysts like Freud, and countless writers.  
- His provocative style and aphorisms made him one of the most quoted philosophers.  
- Controversially appropriated by various political movements, though his actual philosophy opposed dogma.
