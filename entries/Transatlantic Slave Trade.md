## Origins
- Began in the **15th century** with Portuguese traders along Africa’s coast.  
- Expanded in the **16th century** as demand for labor grew in the Americas.  
- Fueled by European colonial expansion and plantation economies.  

## The Triangular Trade
1. **Europe → Africa:** Manufactured goods (guns, textiles, alcohol).  
2. **Africa → Americas (Middle Passage):** Enslaved Africans transported under brutal conditions.  
3. **Americas → Europe:** Raw materials (sugar, cotton, tobacco) produced by enslaved labor.  

## Scale & Impact
- **10–12 million Africans** transported; millions more died during raids or voyages.  
- Major sources: **West and Central Africa**.  
- Destinations: Caribbean, Brazil, North America.  
- Brazil received the largest number (~5 million).  
- Profound demographic, cultural, and economic consequences for Africa and the Americas.  

## Human Cost
- The **Middle Passage** was notorious for overcrowding, disease, and abuse.  
- Mortality rates: 10–20% per voyage.  
- Families and communities devastated in Africa.  
- Enslaved people endured lifelong forced labor and violence.  

## Legacy
- Shaped racial hierarchies and systemic inequalities still present today.  
- Inspired abolitionist movements in the 18th–19th centuries.  
- Remembered as one of history’s greatest crimes against humanity.
