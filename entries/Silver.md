## Introduction
**Silver** is a chemical element with the symbol **Ag** (from Latin *argentum*) and atomic number **47**. It is a precious metal valued for its beauty, conductivity, and industrial applications. Silver has been used for currency, jewelry, and technology for thousands of years.

## History / Origins
- Known since antiquity; mined and used by ancient civilizations in Mesopotamia, Egypt, and Greece.  
- Associated with wealth and trade, often used in coins and ornaments.  
- Played a central role in monetary systems, especially during the Spanish colonial era when vast amounts of silver were extracted from the Americas.  

## Key Features
- **Appearance**: Shiny, white, lustrous metal.  
- **Properties**: Highly conductive (best electrical conductor among metals), malleable, ductile, and resistant to corrosion.  
- **Occurrence**: Found in ores such as argentite and galena, often alongside lead, zinc, and copper.  
- **Uses**:  
  - Jewelry, silverware, and ornamentation.  
  - Currency and investment (coins, bullion).  
  - Electronics and electrical contacts.  
  - Photography (historically, silver halides).  
  - Medicine (antibacterial properties in silver compounds).  
  - Renewable energy (solar panels).  

## Legacy / Impact
- Symbol of purity and wealth across cultures.  
- Central to global trade, especially during the Age of Exploration.  
- Still a major financial asset and industrial resource.  
- Plays a role in modern technology, including electronics and clean energy.
