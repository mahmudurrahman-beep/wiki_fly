## Early Life
- Born: **18 December 1878**, Gori, Georgia (then Russian Empire).  
- Birth name: **Ioseb Besarionis dze Jughashvili**.  
- Adopted the name “Stalin,” meaning “man of steel.”  
- Joined the Bolshevik movement in the early 1900s, engaged in revolutionary activities.  

## Rise to Power
- After Lenin’s death (1924), Stalin outmaneuvered rivals like Trotsky.  
- Became **General Secretary of the Communist Party (1922–1952)**.  
- Consolidated power through purges, propaganda, and control of the party apparatus.  

## Policies
- **Collectivization:** Forced peasants into collective farms, causing famine (notably the **Holodomor in Ukraine**).  
- **Industrialization:** Five-Year Plans transformed the USSR into a major industrial power.  
- **Great Purge (1936–1938):** Mass arrests, executions, and gulag labor camps eliminated perceived enemies.  

## World War II
- Signed the **Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact (1939)** with Nazi Germany, later broken by Hitler’s invasion.  
- Led the USSR in the **Great Patriotic War (1941–1945)**.  
- Key victories: **Battle of Stalingrad (1942–43)**, turning point against Germany.  
- USSR emerged as one of the two global superpowers.  

## Later Years
- Established Soviet dominance in Eastern Europe, beginning the **Cold War**.  
- Cult of personality glorified Stalin as an infallible leader.  
- Died: **5 March 1953**, Moscow, after a stroke.  

## Legacy
- Remembered as both a **builder of Soviet power** and a **brutal dictator**.  
- His rule caused **millions of deaths** through famine, purges, and forced labor.  
- Left a lasting impact on global politics, communism, and 20th‑century history.
