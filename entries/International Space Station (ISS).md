## Origins
- Launched: **November 20, 1998** (first module: Zarya).  
- Built through collaboration between the **United States, Russia, Europe, Japan, and Canada**.  
- Represents one of the largest cooperative engineering projects in history.  

## Structure
- Mass: ~**450,000 kg**.  
- Dimensions: **109 m long**, **73 m wide** (solar arrays).  
- Pressurized volume: ~**1,000 m³**.  
- Orbit: ~**413–422 km altitude**, speed ~**27,600 km/h**, completing **16 orbits per day**.  

## Crew
- Average crew size: **7 astronauts/cosmonauts**.  
- Over **280 astronauts** from 20+ countries have visited.  
- Current commander (Dec 2025): **Mike Fincke (NASA)**.  

## Functions
- **Scientific Research**: Microgravity experiments in biology, physics, medicine, and materials science.  
- **Earth Observation**: Climate monitoring, disaster response, and environmental studies.  
- **Technology Testing**: Preparing for long‑duration missions to the Moon and Mars.  
- **International Cooperation**: Symbol of peaceful collaboration across nations.  

## Milestones
- **2000**: First permanent crew arrived.  
- **2011**: Station declared “complete” after major modules installed.  
- **2025**: Celebrated **25 years of continuous human habitation**.  

## Future
- Planned retirement around **2030**, with commercial space stations expected to take over.  
- Russia is developing its own **Russian Orbital Station (ROS)**, aiming for launch in the late 2020s.
