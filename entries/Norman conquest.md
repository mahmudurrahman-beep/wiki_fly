# Norman Conquest

## Background
- King **Edward the Confessor** died in January 1066 without an heir.  
- Harold Godwinson was crowned king, but William of Normandy claimed Edward had promised him the throne.  
- Other claimants included **Harald Hardrada of Norway**.  

## Key Events
- **Battle of Stamford Bridge (25 Sept 1066):** Harold defeated Hardrada’s invasion.  
- **Battle of Hastings (14 Oct 1066):** William’s Norman army defeated Harold, who was killed in battle.  
- William crowned **King of England** on **Christmas Day 1066**.  
- Resistance continued until 1071, but William consolidated power through castles, land redistribution, and suppression of revolts.  

## Impact
- **Feudal System:** Introduced Norman feudalism, redistributing land to Norman lords.  
- **Language:** Old English blended with Norman French, shaping Middle English.  
- **Church:** Reorganized under Norman influence, with new Romanesque architecture.  
- **Military & Architecture:** Castles (e.g., Tower of London) symbolized Norman control.  
- **Domesday Book (1086):** Comprehensive survey of land and resources.  

## Legacy
- The conquest permanently altered England’s **culture, governance, and language**.  
- It linked England more closely to continental Europe, especially France.  
- Seen as one of the most decisive events in English history.
