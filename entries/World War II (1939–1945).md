# World War II (1939–1945)

**World War II** was a global conflict involving most of the world’s nations, fought between the **Allies** (including the United States, Soviet Union, United Kingdom, China) and the **Axis** (Germany, Italy, Japan).

## Causes
- Aggressive expansion by Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, and Imperial Japan.  
- Unresolved issues from World War I and the Treaty of Versailles.  
- Rise of totalitarian regimes and militarism.

## Major Events
- **1939:** Germany invades Poland; Britain and France declare war.  
- **1941:** Japan attacks Pearl Harbor; the U.S. enters the war.  
- **1942–1943:** Turning points at Midway (Pacific) and Stalingrad (Eastern Front).  
- **1944:** D-Day (Allied invasion of Normandy).  
- **1945:** Germany surrenders (May); atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki; Japan surrenders (September).

## Outcome
- Devastating human cost: tens of millions of military and civilian deaths.  
- Defeat of Axis powers; occupation and reconstruction of Germany and Japan.  
- Creation of the **United Nations** and start of the **Cold War** between the U.S. and USSR.

## Significance
- Redrew global power structures and accelerated decolonization.  
- Advanced technology and warfare, including nuclear weapons.  
- Profound social, political, and economic consequences worldwide.
