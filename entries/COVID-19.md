## Introduction
**COVID-19** is a respiratory illness caused by the **SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus**, first identified in **Wuhan, China** in late 2019. It rapidly spread worldwide, leading to the **COVID-19 pandemic**, one of the most significant global health crises in modern history.

## History / Origins
- First cases reported in December 2019 in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China.  
- Declared a **Public Health Emergency of International Concern** by the World Health Organization (WHO) in January 2020.  
- Declared a **pandemic** on **11 March 2020**.  
- Spread globally, affecting nearly every country and disrupting health systems, economies, and daily life.  

## Key Features
- **Cause**: SARS-CoV-2 virus, a novel coronavirus.  
- **Transmission**: Primarily through respiratory droplets, aerosols, and contact with contaminated surfaces.  
- **Symptoms**: Fever, cough, breathing difficulty, fatigue, loss of taste or smell; severe cases can lead to pneumonia and organ failure.  
- **Treatment**: Supportive care, oxygen therapy, antivirals, monoclonal antibodies.  
- **Prevention**: Vaccination, mask-wearing, hand hygiene, physical distancing.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Triggered unprecedented global response, including lockdowns, travel restrictions, and rapid vaccine development.  
- Vaccines (Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna, AstraZeneca, Sinovac, etc.) were developed in record time.  
- Sparked debates on public health, misinformation, and preparedness for future pandemics.  
- Changed work, education, and social interaction worldwide.
