## Introduction
**AIDS** is a disease caused by the **Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)**. HIV attacks the bodyâ€™s immune system, specifically the CD4 cells (T cells), making individuals vulnerable to infections and certain cancers. Without treatment, HIV infection progresses to AIDS, the most severe stage.

## History / Origins
- First recognized in **1981** in the United States when unusual infections appeared among young men.  
- HIV was identified as the cause in **1983**.  
- Quickly became a global epidemic, especially in sub-Saharan Africa.  

## Key Features
- **Cause**: HIV infection leading to immune system collapse.  
- **Transmission**: Spread through blood, sexual contact, mother-to-child (pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding), and contaminated needles.  
- **Symptoms**: Severe immune deficiency, opportunistic infections (like tuberculosis, pneumonia, Kaposiâ€™s sarcoma), weight loss, fever, night sweats.  
- **Treatment**: Antiretroviral therapy (ART) â€” daily medication that controls HIV, prevents progression to AIDS, and reduces transmission risk.  
- **Prevention**: Safe sex practices, needle exchange programs, HIV testing, and preventive medications (PrEP).  

## Legacy / Impact
- AIDS has claimed over **44 million lives globally** to date.  
- As of 2024, about **40.8 million people** were living with HIV worldwide.  
- Once considered a death sentence, HIV/AIDS is now a **manageable chronic condition** with ART.  
- Sparked major public health campaigns, global cooperation, and awareness of sexual health.
