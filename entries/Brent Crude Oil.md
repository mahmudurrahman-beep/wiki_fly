## What It Is
- **Benchmark crude oil** used globally to price two-thirds of the world’s internationally traded crude.  
- Origin: Extracted from the North Sea (fields like Brent, Forties, Oseberg, Ekofisk).  
- Futures contracts are traded on the **ICE Futures Europe exchange**.

## Importance
- Brent is the **global benchmark**, compared with **WTI (West Texas Intermediate)** in the US.  
- Used by refiners, traders, and governments to set contracts and forecast energy costs.  
- Influences global inflation, shipping costs, and energy policy.  

## Risks & Trade-offs
- **Volatility:** Prices swing with geopolitical events, OPEC decisions, and global demand.  
- **Energy transition:** Long-term demand may decline as renewables expand.  
- **Geopolitical chokepoints:** Events in the Middle East, Russia, or shipping routes (like the Suez Canal) can cause sudden spikes.
