## Geography & Status
- Located in western Saudi Arabia, about 70 km inland from the Red Sea.  
- Population: Over **2 million** residents.  
- Non-Muslims are strictly prohibited from entering the city.  

## Religious Significance
- **Kaaba:** Cube-shaped shrine considered the "House of God." Muslims face it during daily prayers (Qibla).  
- **Masjid al-Haram:** Largest mosque in the world, surrounding the Kaaba.  
- **Zamzam Well:** Sacred spring believed to have miraculously appeared for Hagar and Ishmael.  
- **Hajj:** Annual pilgrimage required once in a lifetime for Muslims who are physically and financially able.  
- **Umrah:** Lesser pilgrimage that can be performed at any time of year.  

## Historical Timeline
- **c. 570 CE:** Birthplace of Prophet Muhammad.  
- **610 CE:** Muhammad receives first revelations of the Qur’an near Mecca.  
- **622 CE:** Muhammad and followers migrate to Medina (Hijra).  
- **630 CE:** Muhammad returns, Mecca becomes the spiritual center of Islam.  
- **Modern Era:** Expanded under Saudi rule with massive infrastructure projects to accommodate pilgrims.  

## Modern Context
- Governed by Saudi Arabia, with strict religious oversight.  
- Annual Hajj draws **2–3 million pilgrims** from around the world.  
- Symbol of unity for Muslims, as all face Mecca during prayer.
