## Early Life
- Born: **29 July 1883**, Predappio, Italy.  
- Background: Son of a socialist father and devout Catholic mother.  
- Worked as a teacher and journalist before entering politics.  

## Rise to Power
- Founded the **Fascist Party (Partito Nazionale Fascista)** in 1919.  
- Exploited post–World War I unrest, promising national revival.  
- **March on Rome (1922):** Mussolini and his followers pressured King Victor Emmanuel III to appoint him Prime Minister.  

## Dictatorship
- Title: *Il Duce* (“The Leader”).  
- Established a **one-party state**, suppressing opposition and controlling media.  
- Promoted nationalism, militarism, and expansionism.  
- Invaded **Ethiopia (1935)**, leading to international condemnation.  

## World War II
- Allied with Hitler and Nazi Germany (Rome-Berlin Axis, 1936).  
- Entered WWII in 1940, but Italy’s military campaigns largely failed.  
- Overthrown in **1943** after Allied invasion of Italy.  
- Rescued by German forces, set up the puppet **Italian Social Republic** in northern Italy.  

## Downfall
- As Germany collapsed in 1945, Mussolini tried to flee.  
- Captured by Italian partisans and executed on **28 April 1945**.  
- His body was displayed publicly in Milan as a symbol of fascism’s fall.  

## Legacy
- Founder of **Fascism**, influencing authoritarian movements worldwide.  
- Remembered for his brutal regime, failed military ventures, and alliance with Hitler.  
- His rule left Italy devastated and discredited fascism as a political system.
