## Introduction
**Coal** is a combustible black or brownish‚Äëblack sedimentary rock formed from ancient plant matter. It is primarily composed of carbon, along with hydrogen, sulfur, oxygen, and nitrogen. Coal has been a major source of energy for centuries, fueling industry, electricity, and transportation.

## History / Origins
- Formed millions of years ago during the **Carboniferous Period** (about 360‚Äì300 million years ago).  
- Used as fuel in China as early as **1000 BCE**.  
- Became central to the **Industrial Revolution** in the 18th and 19th centuries, powering steam engines and factories.  
- Remains one of the most widely used fossil fuels worldwide.

- **Properties**: High energy content, solid fuel, relatively easy to mine.  
- **Uses**:

  - Steel production (coke from coal used in blast furnaces).

## Legacy / Impact
- Fueled industrial growth and economic expansion worldwide.  
- Major contributor to **greenhouse gas emissions** and **climate change**.  
- Many countries are transitioning away from coal toward renewable energy.  
- Still significant in developing economies for affordable energy.
