## Early Life
- Born: **16 January 1901**, Banes, Cuba.  
- Came from a poor family, worked various jobs before joining the army in 1921.  
- Rose to prominence as a sergeant, later becoming a key political figure.

## Political Career
- **1933:** Played a major role in the “Sergeants’ Revolt,” which ousted President Gerardo Machado.  
- **1940–1944:** Elected President of Cuba, introducing a progressive constitution but also consolidating power.  
- **1948–1952:** Served as a senator.  
- **1952 coup:** Seized power before elections, canceling democratic processes and ruling as dictator.

## Dictatorship (1952–1959)
- Aligned closely with U.S. corporations and mafia interests in Havana.  
- His regime was marked by **corruption, repression, and inequality**.  
- Allowed American businesses to dominate Cuban industries, especially sugar and tourism.  
- Suppressed opposition through censorship and violence.

## Downfall
- Growing resistance from Fidel Castro’s **26th of July Movement**.  
- Batista fled Cuba on **1 January 1959**, as Castro’s forces entered Havana.  
- Lived in exile in Portugal and Spain until his death.

## Death
- Died: **6 August 1973**, Marbella, Spain.  
- Buried in Madrid.

## Legacy
- Remembered as a symbol of corruption and dictatorship in Cuba.  
- His downfall paved the way for Castro’s socialist revolution.  
- Seen as both a modernizer (in early years) and a tyrant (in later years).
