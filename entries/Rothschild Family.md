## Introduction
The **Rothschild family** is a prominent European banking dynasty of Ashkenazi Jewish origin. Founded by **Mayer Amschel Rothschild** in Frankfurt in the late 18th century, the family expanded across Europe, becoming synonymous with wealth, finance, and philanthropy.

## History / Origins
- **Founder**: Mayer Amschel Rothschild (1744‚Äì1812), who established a banking business in Frankfurt.  
- His five sons spread across Europe, founding branches in **London, Paris, Vienna, Naples, and Frankfurt**.  
- The family rose to prominence by financing governments, railways, and industrial projects during the 19th century.  

## Key Features
- **Banking Dynasty**: Known for international finance, government loans, and investments in infrastructure.  
- **Noble Titles**: Granted baronial status in Austria (1822) and later in Britain.  
- **Philanthropy**: Supported education, science, and Jewish community projects.  
- **Influence**: Played a major role in European politics and economics during the 19th century.  

## Legacy / Impact
- The Rothschilds became symbols of global finance and wealth.  
- Their name is often associated with conspiracy theories, though historically their influence was rooted in legitimate banking success.  
- Today, Rothschild businesses remain active in banking, investment, and wine production.
