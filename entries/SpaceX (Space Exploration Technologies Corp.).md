## Origins
- Founded: **March 14, 2002**, in California, USA.  
- Founder: **Elon Musk**.  
- Headquarters: **Starbase, Texas**.  
- Mission: To reduce space transportation costs and enable colonization of Mars.  

## Key Achievements
- **Falcon 1 (2008)**: First privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit.  
- **Falcon 9 (2010–present)**: Reusable rocket, cornerstone of SpaceX’s success.  
- **Dragon Capsule (2012)**: First private spacecraft to deliver cargo to the ISS.  
- **Crew Dragon (2020)**: Restored U.S. human spaceflight capability.  
- **Falcon Heavy (2018)**: World’s most powerful operational rocket.  
- **Starship (ongoing)**: Fully reusable spacecraft designed for Moon, Mars, and beyond.  

## Starlink
- Satellite internet constellation with **over 6,000 satellites launched**.  
- Provides broadband service globally, especially in remote areas.  
- Expected to generate billions in revenue annually.  

## Financials
- Revenue (2024): **$13.1 billion**.  
- Operating income (2024): **$4.5 billion**.  
- Employees: ~**13,000**.  
- Preparing for IPO in 2026, targeting valuation of **$1.5 trillion**.  

## Legacy
- SpaceX has become the **world’s leading launch provider**.  
- Pioneered reusable rocket technology, drastically lowering launch costs.  
- Seen as the frontrunner in humanity’s future expansion to Mars.
