## Early Life
- Born: **18 July 1918**, Mvezo, South Africa.  
- Birth name: **Rolihlahla Mandela**; later given the name “Nelson” by a teacher.  
- Studied law at the University of Fort Hare and University of Witwatersrand.  

## Anti-Apartheid Struggle
- Joined the **African National Congress (ANC)** in 1944.  
- Co-founded the **ANC Youth League**.  
- Advocated nonviolent resistance, later supported armed struggle after the Sharpeville Massacre (1960).  
- Arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment in **1964** for sabotage and conspiracy.  
- Spent **27 years in prison**, mostly on Robben Island.  

## Presidency
- Released in **1990** after global pressure and negotiations.  
- Awarded the **Nobel Peace Prize (1993)** with F.W. de Klerk.  
- Elected **President of South Africa (1994–1999)**, leading the transition from apartheid to democracy.  
- Promoted reconciliation through the **Truth and Reconciliation Commission**.  

## Legacy
- Global symbol of **justice, peace, and human rights**.  
- Advocated for education, equality, and HIV/AIDS awareness.  
- Remembered as “**Madiba**,” his clan name, a term of respect and affection.  
- Died: **5 December 2013**, Johannesburg, aged 95.
