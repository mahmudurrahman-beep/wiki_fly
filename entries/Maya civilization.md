## Origins
- Emerged around **2000 BCE** in Mesoamerica.  
- Early villages practiced agriculture (maize, beans, squash).  
- Developed into complex city‑states by the **Classic Period (250–900 CE)**.  

## Geography
- Spread across **Yucatán Peninsula, Chiapas, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, El Salvador**.  
- Major cities: **Tikal, Palenque, Copán, Chichén Itzá, Uxmal**.  

## Society & Culture
- Organized into independent **city‑states**, often ruled by kings.  
- Religion centered on **polytheism**, rituals, and human sacrifice.  
- Developed the **Maya script**, the most sophisticated writing system in pre‑Columbian Americas.  
- Achievements in **mathematics (concept of zero)** and **astronomy (calendar systems)**.  

## Architecture
- Built monumental **pyramids, temples, palaces, and observatories**.  
- Famous structures: **El Castillo at Chichén Itzá**, **Temple of the Great Jaguar at Tikal**.  

## Collapse
- **Classic Maya collapse (~900 CE)**: decline of southern lowland cities due to warfare, drought, and resource depletion.  
- Northern cities like Chichén Itzá continued until Spanish conquest in the **16th–17th centuries**.  
- Final Maya stronghold, **Nojpetén (Petén, Guatemala)**, fell in **1697 CE**.  

## Legacy
- Maya descendants still live across Central America, preserving language and traditions.  
- Contributions to **science, art, and architecture** remain globally significant.  
- Their calendar and astronomical knowledge continue to fascinate scholars.
