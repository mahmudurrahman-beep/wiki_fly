## Background
- Japan had been under the **Tokugawa Shogunate (1603–1868)**, a feudal military government.  
- Western powers pressured Japan to open trade after centuries of isolation (sakoku).  
- Many samurai and reformers believed the shogunate could not protect Japan from foreign domination.  

## Key Events
- **January 3, 1868:** Imperial forces declared the restoration of power to Emperor Meiji.  
- Tokugawa Yoshinobu, the last shogun, resigned but conflict followed (Boshin War, 1868–1869).  
- The emperor moved the capital from Kyoto to **Edo (renamed Tokyo)**.  

## Major Reforms
- **Abolition of feudal domains (han system)** and samurai privileges.  
- Creation of a **centralized government** under the emperor.  
- **Industrialization:** Railways, factories, and modern military built.  
- **Education reforms:** Universal schooling introduced.  
- **Westernization:** Adoption of Western technology, institutions, and even fashion.  

## Outcomes
- Japan became a **modern nation-state**.  
- By the early 20th century, Japan was a major military and industrial power.  
- The Meiji Constitution (1889) established a constitutional monarchy.  

## Legacy
- Seen as the turning point between **traditional Japan** and **modern Japan**.  
- Inspired other Asian nations to modernize.  
- Set the stage for Japan’s rise as a global power in the 20th century.
